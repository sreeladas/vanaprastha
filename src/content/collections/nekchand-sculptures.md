Nine sculptural figures reflecting the influence of Nek Chand, celebrated creator of the Rock Garden in Chandigarh. Characterized by their folk-inspired forms and use of repurposed materials, the figures embody a spirit of intuitive creativity and grassroots artistry. Among them is a female figure beside a fixed well, fondly named Uma by author Ruskin Bond during a visit - an interaction captured in a photograph that remains part of the family's cherished memory.

These sculptures hold personal and emotional significance within the family. Together, they represent not only a distinct artistic style but also a deep personal narrative rooted in memory, interaction, and place.
