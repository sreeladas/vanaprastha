This set of nine sculptural figures reflects the influence of Nek Chand, celebrated creator of the Rock Garden in Chandigarh. Characterised by their folk-inspired forms and use of repurposed materials, the figures embody a spirit of intuitive creativity and grassroots artistry. Among them is a female figure beside a fixed well, fondly named Uma by author Ruskin Bond during a visit — an interaction captured in a photograph that remains part of the family's cherished memory.

These sculptures hold personal and emotional significance within the Vanaprastha family. While their documentation serves as an important step toward preservation and historical recognition, it does not necessarily indicate an immediate donation. Together, the figures represent not only a distinct artistic style but also a deep personal narrative rooted in memory, interaction, and place.
