This section features traditional handcrafted wooden vessels commonly used in Indian households for storage and utility purposes. The rounded pot, often referred to as a "matka," was typically used to store water or grains. The taller cylindrical container served for storing larger quantities of food grains or liquids. These vessels not only reflect utilitarian craftsmanship but also embody the earthy, organic aesthetics of rural India. The visible grain and patina of the wood highlight the handmade quality and age of these timeless artifacts.
